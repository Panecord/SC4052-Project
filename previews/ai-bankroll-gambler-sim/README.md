# 🎰 AI Bankroll Gambler Sim

An interactive poker bankroll management simulator where you play against AI opponents, learn proper risk management, and master variance control.

## 🎮 Features

- **Realistic Bankroll Management**: Track your bankroll, buyin amounts, and risk exposure
- **AI Opponents**: Play against 2 AI villains with realistic decision-making
- **Multiple Stake Levels**: Choose from Micro, Low, Mid, and High stakes
- **Hand Action System**: Fold, Check, Call, Raise, and All-In decisions
- **Session Statistics**: Track win rate (BB/hr), ROI, variance, and profit
- **Risk Assessment Meter**: Visual indicator of your current risk level
- **Real-Time Action Log**: See every action and decision in the hand
- **Beautiful Dark Theme**: Professional poker table UI with smooth animations

## 🚀 Getting Started

1. Open `index.html` in a modern web browser
2. Set your desired stake level using the dropdown
3. Adjust your buy-in amount (default $100)
4. Click "Buy In" to start a hand
5. Make your decisions: Fold, Check, Call, Raise, or All-In
6. Watch the AI respond to your actions
7. Track your bankroll growth and statistics

## 📊 Game Mechanics

### Bankroll Management
- You start with $10,000
- Buy-ins are deducted from your bankroll
- Winning hands add to your bankroll
- Risk meter shows if your stake is healthy (keep BB ≤ 5% of bankroll)

### Stake Levels
- **Micro**: $1/$2 (great for learning)
- **Low**: $5/$10 (building bankroll)
- **Mid**: $25/$50 (default difficulty)
- **High**: $100/$200 (hardcore challenge)

### Player Actions
- **Fold**: Exit the hand, lose your bet
- **Check**: Stay in the hand for free
- **Call**: Match the current bet
- **Raise**: Increase the bet amount
- **All-In**: Push all remaining chips to the pot

### Statistics Tracked
- **Hands Played**: Total hands in session
- **Win Rate**: Big Blinds earned per hour
- **Variance**: Volatility of your results
- **ROI**: Return on investment percentage

## 💡 Strategy Tips

1. **Keep proper bankroll ratios** - Never risk more than 5% of bankroll in a single hand
2. **Play tight early** - Fold more hands early in learning
3. **Position matters** - AI players make different decisions based on situations
4. **Track variance** - Poker has natural swings; don't chase losses
5. **Study the action log** - Learn from AI decisions and your own plays
